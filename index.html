WHAT: Overall Design and Performance Assessment Simulator for CS-25 Scooper Aerial Firefighter

As climate-driven wildfires intensify, legacy scoopers like the CL-415 are aging and out of production. This project explores retrofitting the ATR 72 into the FF72 amphibious scooper as a certifiable, cost-effective solution. HOW: Modular Python-Based 6-DOF Simulation Framework

Utilized XDSM diagrams to visualize modular data flows and flowcharts for each model, enabling precise transient simulations in AEO, OEI (engine failure at critical points), and RTO scenarios under certification constraints.
Incorporated pilot inputs, environmental factors (e.g., ISA deviations, wind shear), and validation against empirical data to quantify retrofit effects on performance and stability.

WHAT: Numerical Investigation and Parametric Study of Subsonic Tandem-Axial Flow Compressor for Aerodynamic Optimization

This thesis, submitted to GITAM (Deemed to be University) in April 2023, addresses high-load compressor design challenges by developing a subsonic tandem-axial flow rotor to enhance stage pressure ratio while mitigating adverse pressure gradients, flow separation, and end-wall losses. Objectives include creating 2D/3D blade geometries using parabolic arc methods, conducting CFD simulations with ANSYS Fluent to analyze flow physics, and performing parametric studies on pitch percentage (PP) and axial overlap (AO) to optimize performance at on/off-design conditions, under Mr. Praveen Kumar Akula's guidance.

HOW: Systematic Design and Numerical Methodology

Designed 2D tandem airfoils and 3D rotors with spanwise variations in stagger, chord, reaction, and diffusion factor using Python scripts based on parabolic arc methods, ensuring solidity 1-1.5 and incorporating AO/PP for boundary layer control.
Implemented CFD setup in ANSYS Fluent with RANS equations, SST k-ω turbulence model, structured hexahedral mesh (y+ <1), grid independence, and convergence criteria (residuals <10^-6) for accurate flow analysis.
Developed Python scripts for parametric analysis, varying PP (70-80%) and AO (-1 to 5%), evaluating metrics like pressure ratio, efficiency, and loss coefficient at on/off-design conditions using formulas for flow angles, solidity, and boundary conditions.

RESULTS: Optimized Performance and Flow Insights

Performance Gains
Tandem rotor achieved 9.22% higher pressure rise, 34.17% better efficiency, and 78.84% lower loss coefficient than conventional design, attributed to gap nozzle flow energizing boundary layers and reducing separation.
Parametric Analysis
Optimal 80% PP and 1% AO configuration increased pressure rise by 1.41% and efficiency by 1.48%, with wider stall-free range and minimized hub separation.
Flow Physics and Contributions
Analysis revealed enhanced boundary layer control, reduced adverse pressure gradients, and mitigated end-wall losses, providing a preliminary design criterion for tandem rotors and Python GUI for flow angle assessment (Appendix A).

WHAT: Modal Analysis of Ariane Payload Fairing

This project involved a detailed modal analysis of the Ariane 5 medium payload fairing (half-model, aluminum shell, free-free boundary conditions) using NASTRAN/PATRAN to characterize natural frequencies and mode shapes. I conducted sensitivity studies on thickness doubling, honeycomb core integration, and I-beam stiffeners to tune the circumferential shell mode to a target 1 Hz frequency, balancing stiffness enhancements with mass constraints for structural integrity during separation. This work highlights my expertise in finite element modeling, dynamic analysis, and optimization strategies critical for aerospace vibration control.

HOW: Finite Element Setup and Sensitivity Studies

Developed a half-model FE setup in PATRAN/NASTRAN with triangular shell elements (12800 elements) under free-free conditions, calculating baseline modes to identify low-frequency issues.
Conducted thickness sensitivity by doubling shell thickness, analyzing stiffness-mass trade-offs via eigenvalue solutions to evaluate frequency gains.
Integrated honeycomb core using equivalent properties, iterating core depth and plate thickness to optimize stiffness-to-mass ratio for target frequency.
Added I-beam stiffeners with non-equidistant placement targeting high-strain regions, assessing localized rigidity enhancements through multiple configurations.

WHAT: Finite Element Analysis of I-Beam Using MSC Nastran and Patran

In this project, I conducted a detailed Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of a steel I-beam subjected to shear loading with a clamped boundary condition, using MSC Nastran for solver computations and Patran for pre- and post-processing. I developed both 1D beam and 2D shell models to evaluate global displacements, local stresses, and model convergence, validating results against classical beam theories like Euler-Bernoulli and Timoshenko. This work demonstrates my proficiency in structural modeling tools, theoretical validation, and mesh sensitivity analysis, essential for aerospace component design where accurate prediction of deformations and stresses ensures safety and efficiency.

I-Beam Cross-Section Geometry

1D Beam Model Setup

HOW: Modeling and Analysis Methodology

Constructed 1D beam model using CBEAM elements in Patran, incorporating Timoshenko theory to account for bending and shear deformations, with clamped conditions at one end and distributed shear load at the other.
Developed 2D shell model with CQUAD4 elements based on Mindlin-Reissner theory, enabling detailed capture of in-plane and out-of-plane behaviors, including membrane and shear effects.
Performed grid independence study, refining meshes to ensure solution stability, demonstrating how finer discretizations improve accuracy in local stress predictions while 1D models suffice for global responses.
Validated FEM outputs against analytical beam theories, highlighting how 1D approaches efficiently model global deflections while 2D captures localized phenomena like shear lag and flange warping.

RESULTS: Displacement and Stress Insights

1D Beam Model Outcomes
The 1D model, based on Timoshenko theory, efficiently captured global deflections including shear effects, with stress distributions highlighting bending-dominated behavior, ideal for quick preliminary assessments in aerospace structures.
2D Shell Model Outcomes
The 2D model, using Mindlin-Reissner theory, provided detailed insights into local effects such as shear lag and flange warping, revealing membrane and shear contributions not visible in 1D analysis.
Validation and Engineering Insights
Models aligned with beam theories, with 1D suited for global efficiency and 2D for localized accuracy; insights suggest 3D extensions for nonlinearities, emphasizing multi-dimensional approaches in aerospace design.

WHAT: Optimizing Laminar Flow for Hybrid/Electric Aircraft

In the MAE107 Applied Aerodynamics course at ISAE-SUPAERO, I designed and executed a study on Natural Laminar Flow (NLF) airfoils for regional hybrid/electric aircraft, focusing on boundary layer transition due to bug contamination at 1%, 3%, 5%, and 10% chord locations. I personally developed a Python script for web scraping and XFOIL automation to analyze the UIUC airfoil database, selecting the NLF-416 for its robust endurance factor (155 at 1% trip) under contamination. Using XFLR-5, I optimized a semi-tapered wing for an ATR 72-class aircraft, achieving a 21% power reduction (1264 kW vs. 1605 kW) and 760 kg battery mass savings for a 1.5 hr mission, advancing sustainable regional aviation.

Semi-Tapered Wing Planform

HOW: Airfoil Selection and Wing Optimization

Developed a custom Python script to scrape UIUC airfoil database and automate XFOIL analysis, selecting NLF-416 for its high endurance factor (155 at 1% trip) and stable flat-peak performance at Re=13M, 6000m altitude, N-factor=9.
Modeled boundary layer tripping at 1%, 3%, 5%, and 10% chord on both surfaces, finding NLF-416 minimizes drag penalties (0.016 savings at 1% trip) due to delayed separation and turbulent reattachment.
Designed a semi-tapered wing with washout and dihedral in XFLR-5 for ATR 72, achieving a max endurance factor of 32 at 4° AoA, outperforming fully tapered wing by reducing lift-induced drag.
Calculated 21% cruise power reduction (1264 kW vs. 1605 kW), saving 350 kWh and 760 kg battery mass for a 1.5 hr mission, using a modified Breguet equation for hybrid propulsion.

RESULTS: Performance and Sustainability Impact

Aerodynamic Efficiency
NLF-416 airfoil maintained a high endurance factor (155 at 1% trip), with minimal drag penalty (0.016 savings vs. ATR 72’s 0.017 at no-trip), ensuring robust performance under contamination.
Power and Mass Savings
Semi-tapered wing reduced cruise power by 21% (1264 kW vs. 1605 kW), saving 350 kWh and 760 kg battery mass for a 1.5 hr mission, enabling hybrid propulsion viability.
Sustainability and Future Potential
Hybrid configuration feasible for 1000 km range; full electrification limited by current battery density. Recommended Krueger elements as insect shields to enhance laminar flow stability.

WHAT: Aircraft Design with Electrically Assisted Battery and EML Concept

In the 1MAE506 Aircraft Design Methods course at ISAE-SUPAERO under Prof. Sebastien Defoort, I spearheaded the conceptual design of an A321-class single-aisle transport aircraft (239 passengers, 2500 NM range, CS-25 compliant) and an innovative electrically assisted variant using battery-powered climb/descent and an Electromagnetic Launcher (EML) for takeoff. Employing analytical methods (Breguet range equation, constraint analysis) and ALCAZAR software, I optimized geometries, stability, and performance, achieving a 15% fuel burn reduction and zero emissions during climb/descent in the innovative design, demonstrating a sustainable solution for next-generation aviation.

Aircraft Geometry

HOW: Iterative Design and Optimization Process

Calculated MTOW (106,300 kg conventional, 125,630 kg innovative) using Breguet range equation, iterating via ALCAZAR’s mass-performance loops to converge at OEW/MTOW ratios of 0.5411 and 0.628, incorporating 15,000 kg battery for innovative design.
Conducted constraint analysis to optimize wing loading (665 kg/m²) and thrust-to-weight (0.37 conventional, 0.17 innovative with EML), ensuring compliance with CS-25 regulations.
Designed geometries (conventional: 166.62m² wing, 47.38m² HTP, 34.1m² VTP; innovative: 219.6m² wing, 63.94m² HTP, 22m² VTP) and validated longitudinal/lateral stability using ALCAZAR.
Analyzed trajectory performance, achieving 75.35 PAX-km/kg (conventional) and 76.91–93.98 PAX-km/kg (innovative), with EML and battery power reducing fuel burn by 15%.

RESULTS: Performance and Sustainability Outcomes

Conventional Aircraft Performance
Optimized MTOW of 106,300 kg, OEW of 57,468 kg, and performance of 75.35 PAX-km/kg over a 2500 NM range, with a 5500 NM ferry range, meeting CS-25 stability and efficiency standards.
Innovative Aircraft Efficiency
Achieved 15% fuel burn reduction (14,868 kg) using EML for takeoff and 15,000 kg battery for climb/descent, delivering 76.91–93.98 PAX-km/kg and a 4770 NM ferry range with zero emissions in key phases.
Sustainability Impact
Eliminated CO₂/NOx emissions and reduced noise by up to 50% (NASA studies) during climb/descent, ideal for urban airports. Battery weight limits range but offers scalability with future technology advancements.
